# Project Status

## Project status

Active research prototype with a filled first-pass historical dataset, exploratory notebooks, a present-day global fragility overlap extension, and scenario analysis.

## Completed

- Comparative historical collapse dataset populated across 15 cases and 174 time-window rows
- Validation, audit, and sanity-review workflow
- Uncertainty-aware exploratory notebooks for factor comparison, intensity, and case/category analysis
- Core findings summary notebook
- Present-day global fragility scoring framework and manual scoring templates
- Current-world baseline scoring draft
- Scenario analysis notebook with editable factor-level scenario values
- Repository cleanup into `raw`, `interim`, `processed`, `backups`, and organized reference subfolders

## In progress

- Human review of high-uncertainty rows
- Refinement of scenario values factor by factor
- Improvement of row-level provenance and source traceability

## Main findings so far

- Severe-collapse rows are most strongly differentiated by territorial loss, political fragmentation, legitimacy crisis, fiscal crisis, urban decline, and internal conflict.
- Terminal-collapse rows are especially associated with high fragmentation, territorial loss, fiscal breakdown, legitimacy crisis, administrative overload, and institutional rigidity.
- Environmental and demographic variables matter in some cases, but their coverage is much less even across the full dataset.
- The present-day global overlap framework currently yields a high-fragility baseline result, but the score is explicitly exploratory and scenario-sensitive.

## Known limitations

- This is a first-pass machine-assisted coding draft, not final historical ground truth.
- Some cases are heterogeneous enough that row-level scores still require careful human revision.
- Several factors remain sparse or uncertain across much of the dataset.
- The present-day extension depends on judgment-based scoring rather than automated live indicators.
